Apple Pie

This is a simple apple pie recipe that has become a Lawrence family tradition over many years

It serves 8 people and is a great dessert to serve at gatherings or celebrations

Tradition

For 26 years, far before I was born, we have been hosting Thanksgiving at our house in Chicago every year. It started in Dallas, when my parents got their first home. Our family, who mostly lived in Wisconsin drove down 16 hours in 1999 to attend the first of a long standing tradition. Then, in 2003, the first of my cousins were born. in 2006, my parents moved the tradition to Chicago, in a condo. Then, it was a tradition of 14, still a cram. In 2006, another cousin was born, then in 2007, my sister, and another cousin, and they kept rolling in. In 2010, me and my brother were born, and in 2011, we moved into the house we live at now. Over the years, more cousins, an aunt married then, then another, dogs got added. My uncle moved to Vegas, my other cousins moved to Salt Lake. in 2022, my family moved to London, but that still didn't stop us. Our family flew 2000 miles to keep the tradition alive. It is now 28 people that we fit into our dining room once a year. We take over my neighbors house, we sleep on couches, chairs, floors, we have created many traditions like poker, a wednesday night dance party, turkey bowl. But one that has been there since 1999, is this apple pie recipe.

Family

It traces back to 1942. My great grandpa Martin spent 4 years building a lake house, "the Chimo Shack" after my moms maiden name, Chmielewski. Every summer since I was born, beyone my memory, we have stayed there. The creaky stairs, toilet that slams, falling apart driveway, makes it feel like home. I have made more memories than I can think of there. My brothers middle name is after my great grandpa, and I always remember the story. When my great grandpa finished the house, he and my great grandma, who only died a few years ago, baked a pie, an apple pie. She wrote down the recipe on a card that we have in our cabinet to this day, and we pull out for thanksgiving. Pie is something that is woven into our family, we are bonded by it, pulled by it.

  • Prep Time:30 minutes
  • Bake Time:60 minutes
  • Serves:6-8
  • Diffuculty:7/10

Pie Dough

2 1/2 cups flour - 315g

1 teaspoon salt

1 1/2 sticks butter, cold, cubed

8 tablespoons ice water, or as needed

Filling

2 1/2 lb granny smith apple -1KG- cored, sliced, peeled

1/2 cup sugar -150 g

1/4 cup brown sugar

2 table spoons flour

1/2 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon cinnamon

1/4 teaspoon nutmeg

1/2 lemon

1 Egg, beaten

Preperation

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, add the flour and salt. Mix with fork until combined
  2. Add in cubed butter and break up into flour with fork. Mixture will still have lumps about pea size
  3. Gradually addthe icewaterand continuetomix until the dough starts to come together. You may not need all of the water, but if the dough is too dry then add more. The dough should not be tacky or sticky
  4. Work the dough together with your hands and turn our onto a surface. Work into a ball and cover in cling wrap. Refrigerate.
  5. Peel the apples, then core and slice
  6. In a bowl, add the sliced apples, sugar, flour, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and juice from lemon
  7. Mix until combined and all apples are coated. Refrigerate
  8. Preheat oven to 375F or 200C
  9. On a floured surface, cut the pie dough in half and roll out both halves until round and about 1/8 inches thick
  10. Roll the dough around the rolling pin and unrollonto a pie dish making sure the dough reaches all edges. Trim extra if neccessary
  11. Pour in apple filling mixture and pat down
  12. Roll the other half of the dough on top
  13. Trim the extra dough from the edges and pinch the edges to create a crimp. Make sure edges are sealed together
  14. Brush the pie with the beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar
  15. Cut four slits in the top of the pie to create a vent
  16. Bake pie for 50-60 minutes or until the crust in goldren grown and no greyish or undercooked pastry remains
